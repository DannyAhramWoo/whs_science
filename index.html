Concept Physics

Physics Notes

A concept-first physics book for upper primary. Each chapter starts from what something actually is, not from formulas.

Chapters

3 Electric Circuits Closed loops, conductors, series and parallel, where the meters go Ready
1 Forces and Friction Contact and non-contact forces, balanced and unbalanced
2 Mass and Weight Why mass stays the same on the Moon but weight does not
4 Magnetism Poles, magnetic materials, electromagnets
5 Floating and Density Upthrust, why heavy ships float, density comparisons
6 Heat Transfer Conduction, convection, radiation, thermal equilibrium
7 Light and Colour Reflection, refraction, filters, how we see colour

How to use this book. Read the concept, study the diagram, then answer the Think and Explain questions out loud before opening the answer. Saying it in full sentences is the test — recognising the answer is not the same as knowing it.

The multi-statement practice matters more than it looks: those questions carry double marks.

Companion material

Hewitt, Conceptual Physics — read alongside each chapter. Do the Think and Explain questions at the end; skip Think and Solve, which is calculation practice you do not need yet.

PhET simulations — build the circuit, watch the bulbs, then come back to the diagram. Concepts stick far better after you have moved something with your own hands.
