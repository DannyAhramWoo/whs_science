How to use this book. Read the concept, study the diagram, then answer the Think and Explain questions out loud before opening the answer. Saying it in full sentences is the test — recognising an answer is not the same as knowing it.

The multi-statement practice matters more than it looks: those questions carry double marks.

Concept-first chapters for upper primary science. Companion reading: Hewitt, Conceptual Physics; Suchocki, Conceptual Chemistry; Campbell & Reece, Essential Biology. Companion simulations: PhET and Earth Space Lab.
